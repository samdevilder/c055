  3. Looking at the frequency matrix, we noted that the first 30 words do not follow Zipf’s law. The increments at which they decrease after the most and second-most frequent words are too small. Zipf’s law indicates that words should appear at half the frequency as the word prior to it. Instead, the difference between the most frequent word and the second-most are half however the third most frequent shows up only 100 times less frequent, which does not equate to half of frequency of the second most frequent word.
